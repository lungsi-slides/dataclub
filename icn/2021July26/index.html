The Problem

Consider the research by Ke et al. (2020)

Ke, H.-Y., Yang, H.-Y., Francis, A. J., Collins, T. P., Surendran, H., Alvarez-Laviada, A., Firth, J. M., & MacLeod, K. T. (2020). Changes in cellular Ca2+ and Na+ regulation during the progression towards heart failure in the guinea pig. The Journal of Physiology, 598(7), 1339–1359. https://doi.org/10.1113/JP277038

Normal Pysiology: Action potential in T-tubules triggers Ca2+ cascade

Purkinje fibers activates action potential from ventricular muscles. The action potential spreads into the T-tubules. This causes opening of L-type Ca channels resulting in Ca2+ influx. The calcium influx triggers opening of the ryanodine receptor causing Ca2+ sparks. The increased cytoplasmic Ca2+ causes muscle contraction. After the ventricular muscle has contracted the cytoplasmic Ca2+ is stored via the SERCA and extruded via the NCX. The excess Na+ in the cytoplasm is expelled by the Na-K-ATPase. Finally, the resting state is attained.

Left ventricle hypertrophy: As hypertrophy progresses what does the kinetic of cytoplasmic Ca2+ look like?

Ca2+ transient: Is it different? How is it different? What does it mean?
Compared to control the amplitude of Ca2+ at compensated stage is larger. But, as the hypertrophy progresses the amplitude decreases significantly. Decrease in amplitude indicates slow removal of cytosol Ca2+.

Are any transporter responsible for the slow removal of cytosol Ca2+?

Looking at the time constants for SERCA, Na-Ca exchanger (NCX) and other miscellaneous transporters. At compensated stage the time constants remain unchanged. However, as the hypertrophy progresses, at 150 days the time constant for SERCA makes a significant decline Thus, suggesting reduced rate of removing cytosol Ca2+ by SERCA. Also, the Ca2+ content in the sarcoplasmic reticulum is significantly reduced at 150 days post-AC. The consequence of these two reasons is the rise in cytosol Ca2+ in severe hypertrophy.

What about Ca2+ sparks?

Ca2+ sparks initiate cardiac muscle contraction but it also plays a role in maintaining cytosol Ca2+ concentration. The spark frequency increases at 150 AC. This implies increased Ca2+ SR stores therefore resulting in increased Ca2+ cytosol. Multiplying the amount of spark frequency by the amount of spark mass the authors conlcuded increased spark mediated SR leak.

What could explain the results seen during compensated stage of hypertrophy?

The increased positive inotrophy, the high Ca2+ transient amplitude and the increased sarcoplasmic reticulum Ca2+ content.

What is the pump activity after reactivation?

The Na-K-ATPase when active expells 3Na+ in exchange for 2K+ inflow. Thus, the net current is 1Na+ outflow. The pump activity can therefore be described by the rate of decay of the Na-K-ATPase current. When the cardiac hypertrophy progresses at 150AC the rate of decay is slower, that is, low rate of expelling cytosol Na+. If the area under the curve is a measure of the amount of extruded Na+, then the area for 150AC is smaller. Hence, the amount of extruded Na+ is decreased in hypertrophy.
What could explain for the decreased Na-K-ATPase current and the decreased Na+ outflow?

Is the amount of pump protein decreased during hypertrophy?

As the hypertrophy progresses to 150AC the fluorescence for α-1 & α-2 isoforms are decreased. Furthermore, Western blotting showed lowered levels of α-1 isoform (but not α-2 isoform) and lowered levels of the pump regulator phospholemman (PLM) — the PLM decline is lesser at 150AC.

Could the 50% reduction in pump current during compensated stage be a mechanism for preserving contractility function?

When the Na-K-ATPase current is reduced we observe increase in Ca2+ transient amplitude. The Na+/Ca2+ exchange current is also increased.
Hypothesis: During compensated stage the inhibition of Na-K-ATPase current increases SR load (Ca2+ SR store) and consequently maintains Ca2+ transient.

Dimensional Analysis

What is a dimension?

It is simply a tag we attach to a quantity in an equation expressing some physical law... [D]imensions are only symbols of an elementary algebra, involving neither addition nor subtraction. To ask what are the true or natural dimensions of a quantity makes no more sense than to ask what is the true or natural word for goldfish. (McNish, 1957)

Why are there seven base dimensions?

[F]or the simplest mechanical quantities we need four dimensions to avoid ambiguities, five, if we include solid angle; that if we set one of these quantities equal to unity we can get along with four. But having one quantity equal to unity we cannot set another equal to unity without creating an ambiguity. Thus, unity itself becomes like a dimension, so again we may say we have five. Heat and electromagnetism add at least two more required dimensions. So I might venture to say that we should have seven elemental dimensions, at least, but I do not know, because I do not comprehend all of physics. One, of course, may get along with fewer dimensions if he will tolerate some ambiguities. (McNish, 1957)

Can we also reduce all units to a few "absolute" units?

The symbols for the dimensions follow the rules of algebra but units are not bound by algebraic rules. Therefore, provided that the parent equation is not violated one may set as may quantities equal to 1 thus reducing it to a few "absolute" units. However, this is not recommended. Because,

  • measuring many units require difficult experiments
  • measurement must by accurate
The magnitudes of quantities are not determined by nature, but by the units we have arbitrarily chosen for our measurement system.(McNish, 1957)

What is dimensional analysis?

Dimensional analysis is purely algebraic whose theory is the mathematical theory of dimensionally homogeneous functions, a special class of general types of mathematical functions.

What is a dimensionless product?

If x meters, y seconds and z kilometers are quantities with dimensions, i.e. dimensional quantities, then x meters ⋅ y seconds is a dimensional product. However, x meters ÷ z kilometers is a dimensionless products.

Therefore, a dimensionless product is more than just a scalar it still carries the dimensional information of various quantities it is a product of.

Why study dimensionless products?

Can provide complete information in a compact form.

Example: Drag of a body immersed in stream of incompressible fluid

It is fair to consider this system to be made up of five variables such that F = f(V, D, ρ, μ).

Dimensional analysis to transform the hypothetical expression.

F = f(V, D, ρ, μ) can be transformed into
F ÷ (ρV^2D^2) = f1( (VDρ) ÷ μ ).

Notice,

  • the equation is dimensionally homogeneous
  • left hand side and right hand side are both dimensionless
  • f1 is an unknown function
  • the four variables are compounded into a single independent variable of f1

Substituting for some parameters and constants ((VDρ) ÷ μ → (VD) ÷ (μ ÷ ρ) ÷ R, Reynolds number) this can be reduced for a spherical body with area (1/4)πD^2 to

F = (1/2)CρV^2A

where C = (8/π)f1(R), the drag coefficient.

Experimental graph from the derived relationship


This graphs contains information for
  • drag forces on smooth spherical bodies of
    • all sizes
  • in an incompressible fluid with
    • any density,
    • any viscosity and
    • any speed of flow

Two important properties of dimensionless graphs

  • can provide much more information than graphs having dimensions
  • points in the graph can be determined experimentally.

Softwares that deal with dimensions

diman

diman© is my in-house Clojure based software for dimensional analysis. By default it is based on a seven dimensional system built on the seven base dimensions: [M], [L], [T], [K], [A], [mol] and [cd] representing the quantities mass, length, time, thermodynamic temperature, electric current, amount of substance and luminous instensity respectively.
diman© is capable of performing dimensional consistency checks and derive dimensionless products. The program aims at simplifying the tedious computational steps particularly while deriving dimensionless products.

Getting dimensionless products using diman©

In diman© the user goes through a minimum of two setups (here three is shown for clarity): defining the independent variables of the f in terms of base dimensions, temporarily placing the definitions in standard_formula and defining the independent variables in terms of their dimensional formula. Without the last step the dimensional formula for respective independent variable cannot be obtained for deriving the dimensionless products.
Now the user can compound the processes for generating the dimensional matrix, solving the homogeneous equation and determining the solution matrix in a single code block. Notice that we get the same solution matrix and therefore the same set of dimensionless products.

A few rules of thumb on deciding the dimensional independent variables

  • it must affect the phenomenon that is being studied
  • ommitting variables that logically influence the phenomenon can lead to impasse in calculation or worse, erroneous result
  • things that are practically constants can be considered a variable
  • understand the problem well enough to explain why and how the variable influences
  • have a theory (even if it is crude) of the mechanism of the phenomenon
